# YKI Test for Finnish (Suomi) - Structure and B1-B2 Level Requirements

## Overview

The **YKI (Yleinen Kielitutkinto / National Certificate of Language Proficiency)** is Finland's standardized language proficiency test administered by the Finnish National Agency for Education (Opetushallitus). It assesses functional language proficiency in real-life situations and is recognized for:

- Finnish citizenship applications
- Employment requirements
- Academic purposes
- Professional qualifications

The test is based on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) and evaluates practical language skills rather than theoretical knowledge.

## Test Structure

The YKI intermediate test consists of **four separately scored subtests**, each weighted equally:

### 1. Reading (Lukeminen)
- **Content**: Comprehension tasks based on texts such as advertisements, articles, instructions, and informational materials
- **Text Length**: 400–600 words at B1–B2 level
- **Skills Tested**: Understanding main ideas, details, and inference from written texts

### 2. Writing (Kirjoittaminen)
- **B1 Level**: Short compositions of 80–120 words (e.g., emails, simple messages)
- **B2 Level**: Longer compositions of 120–150 words (e.g., complaint letters, formal correspondence)
- **Focus**: Clarity and communication effectiveness are prioritized over grammatical perfection

### 3. Listening (Kuullunymmärtäminen)
- **Content**: Audio clips including conversations, announcements, and spoken instructions
- **B1 Level**: Features slower, clearer speech
- **B2 Level**: Natural pace with varied accents and more complex discourse
- **Skills Tested**: Understanding spoken Finnish in various contexts

### 4. Speaking (Puhuminen)
- **Format**: One-on-one interview with an examiner
- **Tasks**: Describing pictures, role-playing scenarios (e.g., booking appointments, making requests), expressing opinions
- **B1 Level**: Basic fluency and ability to handle everyday situations
- **B2 Level**: Greater detail, more complex expressions, and ability to discuss abstract topics

**Test Duration**: 5 hours for intermediate level (including breaks)

## B1-B2 Level Requirements

### B1 Level (Independent User - Grade 3)

**Language Skills:**
- Handle daily life tasks: shopping, travel, casual conversation
- Understand and produce simple texts about familiar topics
- Express basic opinions and describe experiences

**Grammar Requirements:**
- Present tense (e.g., "Minä puhun suomea" = I speak Finnish)
- Past tense (imperfekti and perfekti)
- Basic sentence structures
- Common cases (nominatiivi, genetiivi, partitiivi, inessiivi, elatiivi)

**Vocabulary:**
- Approximately 2,000 words
- Sufficient for basic emails, small talk, and everyday communication

**Practical Applications:**
- Basic workplace communication
- Simple academic tasks
- Daily social interactions

### B2 Level (Upper Intermediate - Grade 4)

**Language Skills:**
- Manage work or academic topics
- Discuss plans and express detailed opinions
- Handle longer, more complex texts
- Participate in debates and discussions

**Grammar Requirements:**
- All B1 grammar plus:
- Conditionals (e.g., "Jos olisin rikas" = If I were rich)
- Additional cases (ablatiivi, adessiivi, allatiivi, illatiivi, translatiivi)
- More complex sentence structures
- Subordinate clauses

**Vocabulary:**
- Approximately 4,000 words
- Ability to understand and use more specialized terminology
- Comfortable with abstract concepts

**Practical Applications:**
- University programs in Finnish
- Professional roles requiring Finnish
- Complex workplace communication
- Academic writing and reading

## Scoring System

- Each subtest is scored separately on the CEFR scale
- **Grade 3** = B1 level
- **Grade 4** = B2 level
- **Grade 5** = C1 level (advanced)

## Citizenship Requirements

For Finnish citizenship applications (submitted on or after January 8, 2026):
- **Grade 3 (B1 level)** is required
- Must pass **one oral subtest** (speaking) AND **one written subtest** (writing, listening, or reading)
- Acceptable combinations:
  - Speaking + Writing
  - Speaking + Listening
  - Speaking + Reading

## Test Preparation Tips

1. **Practice all four skills** equally - each subtest is scored separately
2. **Focus on practical communication** rather than perfect grammar
3. **Build vocabulary** systematically (2,000 words for B1, 4,000 for B2)
4. **Practice with authentic materials**: Finnish newspapers, radio, TV shows
5. **Time management**: Practice completing tasks within the allocated time
6. **Speaking practice**: Engage in conversations, describe pictures, role-play scenarios

## Resources

- **Registration**: Available through authorized test centers throughout Finland
- **Test Dates**: Multiple test dates available throughout the year
- **Required Documents**: Valid official identity document (passport, identity card) on test day
